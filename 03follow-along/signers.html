Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

John Hancock
Perhaps the most famous signer, known for his distinctively large, bold signature. As president of the Continental Congress, he signed first and reportedly said he wrote it large so "the King of England can read that without spectacles."
Benjamin Franklin
Already a renowned scientist, inventor, and diplomat by 1776, Franklin was one of the oldest signers at age 70 and brought immense credibility to the cause.
John Adams
A passionate advocate for independence who later became the second U.S. president. He and Thomas Jefferson famously both died on July 4, 1826 - the 50th anniversary of the Declaration.
Samuel Adams
John's cousin and a key organizer of the Boston Tea Party, he was one of the most vocal early advocates for American independence.
Thomas Jefferson
Though primarily remembered as the principal author of the Declaration, he was also one of its signers representing Virginia.
John Witherspoon
The only clergyman to sign, he was president of what is now Princeton University and mentor to James Madison.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The only Catholic signer and reportedly the wealthiest, he lived the longest of any signer, dying in 1832.

These men risked their lives, fortunes, and sacred honor by putting their names to what the British Crown considered an act of treason. As Franklin reportedly quipped, "We must all hang together, or assuredly we shall all hang separately."
